TradeAid Integrated Calls for Support for Basket Weavers Ahead of BICAF

Nicholas Apokerah, Executive Director of TradeAid Integrated, an NGO based in Bolgatanga, Ghana, is calling for support for basket weavers and other arts and crafts producers in Bolgatanga ahead of the Bolgatanga International Craft and Arts Fair (BICAF) slated for November 1-5, 2023.

BICAF is an annual event that showcases the best of Bolgatanga's arts and crafts, including baskets, pottery, textiles, and wood carvings.

The fair attracts visitors from all over Ghana and the world, and it is a major opportunity for local artisans to sell their products and promote their work.

Mr. Apokerah is urging people to support basket weavers and other arts and crafts producers by buying their products and attending BICAF.

He says that the arts and crafts sector is an important part of the Bolgatanga economy, and it provides employment and income for many people in the community.

"The arts and crafts sector is a vital part of the Bolgatanga economy. It provides employment and income for many people in the community. That's why it's so important to support our basket weavers and other arts and crafts producers," the Executive Director of TradeAid Integrated said.

Mr. Apokerah also says that BICAF is a great opportunity for people to learn about Bolgatanga's culture and heritage. The fair features cultural performances, fun activities, and a variety of food and drinks.

"BICAF is a great way to learn about Bolgatanga's culture and heritage. It's also a great opportunity to buy unique and beautiful handicrafts from local artisans. I encourage everyone to come out and support BICAF this year," Nicholas Apokerah said.
